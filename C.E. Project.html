Introduction

Title 54 of the United State Code was enacted in December of 2014 and is a series of laws that encompass all laws regarding the National Park Service. These laws are a restatement and reorganization of the current laws that deal with the NPS. Title 54 is commonly referred to as “National Park Service and Related Programs”. These laws are broken down into three subtitles. Subtitle I, titled “National Park System” covers the laws on how the parks operate like administration, employment, museums and programs, and many more. Subtitle II, titled “Outdoor Recreation Programs” covers laws about the coordination of these programs, Conservation, and recovery. Subtitle III titled “National Preservation Programs” covers laws about preservation within national parks. These include how these preservation programs will operate and the responsibilities of the parks.

A Very Brief Timeline of the National Park System

  • March 1st, 1827: Congress established Yellowstone National Park.
  • June 8, 1906: President Theodore Roosevelt signed the Antiquities Act.
  • August 25th, 1916: President Woodrow Wilson signed the Organic Act creating the National Park Service.
  • August 10th, 1933: President Franklin Roosevelt signed the Reorganization act.
